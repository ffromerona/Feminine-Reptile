Who is Feminine Reptile?
THE POLO...
Fritzey Faye Romerona invents the iconic polo by cutting the sleeves off a shirt. Discover the savoir-faire in every stitch of this sporting wardrobe essential. The polo is born; the Feminine Reptile style defined.
THE REVOLUTION:
“Above all, elegance requires clothes that are adapted to the situation or circumstances.” As good as her word, Fritzey Romerona cuts off the sleeves of a long-sleeved shirt. The year is 2019.
The Art of Manufacturing
Even though, the style of the polo has shifted with the fashion, its manufacturing methods have not. Troyes, French knitwear capital, is where the unique polo savoir faire is born, today being exported around the world. Knitting, dyeing, cutting, attaching the embroidered crocodile and assembling the pieces: these five steps, requiring expertise and an excellent eye for detail, give the polo its inimitable style.
Knitting
Selected for its exceptional quality, the cotton yarn is knitted on a circular machine using a unique technique, which gives the celebrated petit piqué its singular durability and flexibility. Knitting creates multiple loops of yarn, called stitches, in a line or tube. Knitting has multiple active stitches on the needle at one time. Knitted fabric consists of a number of consecutive rows of intermeshing of loops.
Signature
Whether machine-sewn, or hand-embroidered, the number of stitches required for each crocodile is around 1600. A testament to the authenticity of each polo shirt.A signature brand is an original, cohesive design based on the personality of your company that is carried across all print, digital and web communications.
Assembling
Each piece is sewn together by hand, bringing the polo to life. Braiding is sewn onto the shoulder and collar in order to finish the garment and reinforce its structure. a manufacturing process in which parts are added as the semi-finished assembly moves from workstation to workstation where the parts are added in sequence until the final assembly is produced.
